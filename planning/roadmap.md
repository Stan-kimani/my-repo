\# Project brief — RemoteKind



\## What

RemoteKind is a premium operator-placement service: we match overwhelmed founders

with vetted remote operators (VAs / ops support) on a fixed monthly retainer, so

the founder gets their time and focus back. The website's job is to sell the

\*service and the trust behind it\* — not to list tasks. Every page sells freedom

and leverage; the vetting process and operator bench are the product.



\## Who

\*\*The Overwhelmed Solo Entrepreneur / Expert Founder (6–7 figure revenue).\*\*

Coaches, speakers, online-business owners, and small-business operators who have

the money to pay premium rates but are trapped working \*in\* their business — stuck

on email, invoices, and calendars instead of growth.



Core psychological trigger: they don't want "a virtual assistant." They want their

time and peace of mind back. Copy promises freedom and leverage, never a task list.



Their #1 unspoken objection (because this is placement, not personal): \*"How do I

know I'll get someone actually good, and not another bad hire I have to manage?"\*

The site must answer this before they'll book.



\## Success looks like

\- A visitor understands within seconds who RemoteKind helps and what problem it solves.

\- They believe the vetting is real — the "how do I get someone good?" fear is answered.

\- They click through to How It Works or Contact instead of bouncing.

\- \*\*Primary conversion:\*\* they book a discovery call (or join the email list as a

&#x20; secondary, lower-commitment path).

\- They feel safe reaching out because the process, the founder story, and client

&#x20; results all signal a premium, low-risk service.



Primary CTA across the whole site: \*\*Book a call.\*\* Secondary: \*\*Join the list.\*\*



\## In scope (v1) — 4 pages

1\. \*\*Home\*\* — problem → promise (time/leverage) → how it works (3 steps) → proof

&#x20;  (testimonials section) → book-a-call CTA. Testimonials live here, not on their

&#x20;  own page.

2\. \*\*How It Works\*\* — the engagement model + the vetting/quality guarantee. This is

&#x20;  the trust page: how operators are screened, how matching works, what the retainer

&#x20;  includes, what happens if a match isn't right. Replaces a task-listing "Services" page.

3\. \*\*About\*\* — RemoteKind's story and the founder (Mary) as the person who built the

&#x20;  vetting bar. Face + credibility. Why this exists, why the standard is high.

4\. \*\*Contact\*\* — booking link (primary), plus email and a short form. This is where

&#x20;  pricing is discussed, on the call — not published.



Integrations wired in v1: appointment booking, an email-capture form (lead magnet),

and a contact form.



\## Explicitly out of scope (v1)

\*The most important section. Agents build whatever isn't ruled out.\*

\- \*\*No public rates/pricing page.\*\* Pricing is quoted on the discovery call. (Revisit

&#x20; only if a productized self-serve tier is ever added.)

\- \*\*No blog.\*\* Deferred until there's capacity to maintain it; an unfed blog is a

&#x20; liability, not authority.

\- \*\*No standalone testimonials page.\*\* Homepage section only until proof volume justifies more.

\- \*\*No client login / portal / dashboard.\*\* Not a v1 concern.

\- \*\*No online payments / checkout.\*\* Retainers are set up off-site after the call.

\- \*\*No operator-application flow\*\* on the public site (recruiting the bench is a

&#x20; separate funnel, not part of the client-facing site).

\- \*\*No live chat, no custom CMS build.\*\* Content collections (markdown) are enough.



\## Constraints

\- \*\*Voice:\*\* premium, calm, confident. Sells leverage and peace of mind. Never

&#x20; commoditized "tasks I can do for you" language.

\- \*\*Colors:\*\* blue, green, and neutrals — trust, calm, professionalism.

\- \*\*Must integrate with:\*\* appointment booking (e.g. Cal.com / Calendly / TidyCal),

&#x20; an email-marketing form (provider TBD), lead-magnet capture. Pick providers before

&#x20; building the Contact page.

\- \*\*Brand assets:\*\* logo / final palette / founder photo — status TBD (confirm what

&#x20; exists before the About and Home builds).

\- \*\*Budget/time:\*\* TBD.



\## Open items to confirm before copy is written (do not fabricate these)

\- Real client results / testimonials (names, outcomes, permission to use).

\- Founder bio facts and a usable photo.

\- The exact 3-step "how it works" process wording.

\- Chosen booking + email providers.

\- Any real vetting stats (e.g. "top X% of applicants") — only if true.
